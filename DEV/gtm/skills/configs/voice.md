## Channel scope

LinkedIn ONLY, via HeyReach. No email in this campaign. Two assets per target:
1. **Connection request note** — ≤300 characters (LinkedIn hard limit), 2-3
   sentences, no CTA beyond the implicit "accept".
2. **Post-accept follow-up message** — fires only after acceptance, ≤5 short
   sentences, references the same anchor, soft meeting ask allowed here.

## The verified CR structure (Nancy Tang pattern, accept + meeting offer 2026-05-12)

Every CR note does these four things IN THIS ORDER:
1. **Friendly opener:** "Hi {{firstName}}," — no preamble, no "Saw [trigger]".
2. **Clarion capability + instrument mix, capability-FIRST:** lead with what
   Clarion delivers and the instruments it covers, framed as OUR coverage,
   never as their situation. "our Clarion API delivers live risk and P&L
   across rates, FX, credit, and equity derivatives".
3. **Recipient-domain tie:** one clause connecting that coverage to their
   SEAT, drawn from their title/function — never a firm-specific program.
   "the exact mix a global macro book runs on".
4. **Soft compare-notes close on a specific topic:** "Would value connecting
   to compare notes on [specific topic in their world]." Never ask for a
   meeting in the CR. Never "Worth comparing notes?" (too transactional).

Why capability-first: leading with the prospect's situation reads as
surveillance (verified failure: Talcott CRO publicly refuted a note that led
with his firm's workflow and misnamed an incumbent system, 2026-05-11).

## Tone markers

- Peer in finance, not vendor. Coremont was built inside Brevan Howard;
  one institutional professional writing to another.
- Possessive present tense: "our Clarion PMS delivers", "Clarion gives".
- Declarative shared knowledge: "the exact mix a [their kind of book] runs on".
- Read it aloud. If a real person wouldn't send it, rewrite.

## Hard prohibitions (validator-enforced)

- **No incumbent system names on LinkedIn. Ever.** Not Aladdin, Geneva, Eze,
  Enfusion, Murex, Calypso, Imagine, Charles River, Bloomberg AIM/PORT, SS&C,
  AXIS, Clearwater — none, under any circumstance. (Tier 4 is email-only per
  the source skill, and there is no email in this campaign.)
- **No em dashes.** Use a comma, period, or colon.
- **Banned phrases:** "I wanted to reach out", "I hope this finds you well",
  "I'd love to connect", "circle back", "touch base", "leverage", "synergy",
  "solution", "cutting-edge", "revolutionary", "best-in-class",
  "thought leader", "innovative platform", "leading provider",
  "robust solution", "Worth comparing notes?", "no agenda",
  "peers have shared their playbook", "other funds have shared".
- **Never start with** "Hi [Name], I'm Alex from Coremont."
- **Never recite their positions/filings/holdings.** Strategy shape and
  category, yes ("credit relative value with CDS overlays"); catalog and
  weights, never.
- **Scale vs new-capability:** AUM growth, block deals, more-of-the-same are
  SCALE triggers. Never imply an established program is new.
- **Stats verbatim from clarion-capabilities.md §1 only** (e.g. "1,300+
  Clarion users"). No invented benchmarks.

## Anchor rule for LinkedIn

Tier 1 (instruments, from the signal/fund record) + Tier 2 (named Clarion
capability traceable to clarion-capabilities.md). Nothing else. The signal
payload carries the evidence; the fund record carries strategies and the
Clarion coverage match.
